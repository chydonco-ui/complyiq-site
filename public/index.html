The problem

Fragmented, fast‑moving, high‑risk.

Nigerian institutions juggle overlapping mandates from CBN, SEC, NAICOM, NDIC, NFIU, NCC, FIRS and state‑level bodies. Updates are frequent, formats inconsistent, and enforcement is tightening.

  • Manual monitoring of circulars, guidelines and directives.
  • Slow, spreadsheet‑driven impact analysis and implementation.
  • High risk of missed obligations, delayed responses and sanctions.
  • Compliance teams stuck in firefighting instead of strategic risk management.
Our solution

Automated Regulatory Intelligence + Change Management.

ComplyIQ becomes your single source of regulatory truth—monitoring, interpreting and operationalising every relevant change across Nigeria’s regulatory ecosystem.

  • Real‑time ingestion of regulatory updates from all key Nigerian regulators.
  • AI‑powered extraction of obligations, deadlines, thresholds and penalties.
  • Automated workflows that assign owners, track progress and capture evidence.
  • Regulator‑ready reporting and dashboards for executives and boards.
How it works

A five‑layer architecture built for Nigerian regulation.

1. Regulatory ingestion

Structured ingestion of circulars, guidelines, directives, sanctions lists and notices from CBN, SEC, NAICOM, NDIC, NFIU, NCC, FIRS and more.

  • Source registry and connectors for web, PDF, email and APIs.
  • Document normalisation and version tracking.

2. Intelligence (ARI)

NLP and a regulatory knowledge graph transform raw text into structured obligations and relationships.

  • Obligation extraction and classification by topic, sector and product.
  • Impact analysis across products, business units and controls.

3. Change management (RCM)

Every regulatory change becomes a trackable, auditable workflow inside your organisation.

  • Tasks, approvals and escalations mapped to roles and departments.
  • Evidence capture for policies, controls, tests and sign‑offs.

4–5. Reporting & experience

Executive‑grade visibility and seamless integration into your existing systems.

  • Dashboards, KRIs and regulator‑specific views.
  • APIs into core banking, HR, KYC and ticketing tools.
